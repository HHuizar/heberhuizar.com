About

Two professional
identities.

I work at the intersection of marine mammal protection, geophysical survey operations, and environmental compliance. My offshore career spans Tiers 1 through 3 under NMFS guidelines, with real-time mitigation responsibilities across seismic, AUV, and multibeam survey programs. Over 2,000 offshore days, across basins in the Gulf of Mexico, Atlantic, Pacific, and West Africa.

In parallel, I hold a Master's in International Environmental Management (M.A.I.A.) from El Colegio de la Frontera Norte (COLEF), where my thesis evaluated urban park distribution in Tijuana using GIS and an environmental justice framework. That research produced two peer-reviewed book chapters and a set of applied reports for the Observatorio Turístico de Baja California.

The field camera follows me on both tracks. Years of watching the ocean from a survey vessel — where patience and precision are the only instruments that matter — carry directly into wildlife photography.
